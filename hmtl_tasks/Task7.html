Key Concepts

Understanding Autism As a Learning Disability Focuses On The Most Helpful Tips and Teaching Methodologies.

Key concepts in Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) include difficulties with social communication and interaction (e.g., understanding nonverbal cues, forming relationships) and restricted, repetitive patterns of behavior, interests, or activities (e.g., rigid routines, intense interests, sensory sensitivities). Symptoms generally appear in early childhood, though they may become more apparent later when social demands increase. The term "spectrum" highlights the wide variation in the abilities and needs of autistic people, who may experience varying levels of support requirements and can have different strengths and challenges.

Many autistic people are highly empathetic and have a strong moral compass. They may become distressed by injustice and motivated to change the world for the better. Plenty of autistic people are not afraid to stand up for their beliefs and challenge things they see as unjust. .
